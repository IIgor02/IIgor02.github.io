About

Research profile and fields.

I am an M.A. student in Economics at CAEN/UFC. My research interests are in Econometrics, Poverty and Inequality, and Development Economics, with a focus on distributional methods, policy evaluation, regional development, public finance, and macro-financial risk.

My current work combines methodological and applied research. On the econometric side, I study panel-data methods for distributional objects, including weighted-average quantile functionals and extreme panel quantiles under common shocks. On the applied side, I work on poverty, inequality, credit, fiscal capacity, climate-policy targeting, food-price incidence, and financial-stress measurement in Brazil.

I hold a B.A. in Economics from the Federal University of Ceará, where I graduated summa cum laude. My research uses administrative data, survey microdata, panel methods, shift-share designs, regression discontinuity, text-based measurement, and forecasting tools to study policy-relevant questions in developing economies.

Selected Research

Papers under review or with external presentation acceptance.

Distributional Functionals in Fixed-Effects Panels: A Weighted-Average Quantile Approach

Under Review Econometrics Panel Data Quantile Methods

Develops weighted-average quantile regression for fixed-effects panels when the target is an integrated feature of the conditional distribution, such as expected shortfall, lower-tail welfare, inequality-sensitive welfare, or distributional spread.

Common-Shock-Robust Inference for Extreme Panel Quantiles

Under Review Econometrics Extreme Quantiles Common Shocks

Proposes an inference framework for extrapolated extreme panel quantiles that accounts for synchronized common shocks with heterogeneous exposure across units, with an empirical illustration using food-inflation risk.

Text-Based Financial Stress and Sovereign Risk in Brazil

Accepted for presentation, Encontro Brasileiro de Finanças with Victor L. Stilita and Ramon Lima Ribeiro Financial Stress Text Analysis

Develops text-based indicators of financial stress for Brazil using Google Trends and financial news, and evaluates their predictive content for Brazil’s 5-year sovereign CDS spread.

Who Bears the Burden of Monetary Tightening? Credit Default Responses Across Income Groups and Firm Sizes in Brazil

Under Review Macro-Finance Credit Default Heterogeneity

Studies heterogeneous monetary transmission to credit default in Brazil using disaggregated Central Bank data by income bracket, firm size, and state.

From Climate-Vulnerability Indicators to Environmental Policy Targeting: Classification Rules and Public Resource Allocation in Northeast Brazil

Under Review Climate Policy Targeting Public Resource Allocation

Examines how climate-vulnerability indicators are converted into priority groups and whether those groups align with public resource allocation channels in Northeast Brazil.

Other Research

Additional submitted and under-review manuscripts.

Sectoral Productive Shocks and Poverty Reduction in Brazilian Microregions

Under Review Poverty Shift-Share Design Regional Development

Estimates the reduced-form relationship between microregional exposure to national sectoral wage-bill shocks and poverty reduction in Brazil between the 2000 and 2010 censuses.

Nowcasting Brazilian Quarterly GDP: Mixed-Frequency Econometric and Machine Learning Models Under Multiple Performance Criteria

Under Review Forecasting MIDAS Machine Learning

Evaluates a real-time nowcasting framework for Brazilian quarterly GDP using mixed-frequency econometric models, machine-learning methods, and multiple forecast-performance criteria.

Intersectional Lower-Tail Wage Rigidity in Brazil: Evidence from Census Data and Synthetic Cohorts

Submitted, ANPEC Regional Labor Economics Inequality Quantile Regression

Documents intersectional lower-tail wage disadvantage in Brazil using the 2010 Census, quantile methods, RIF-based decompositions, and PNAD Contínua synthetic cohorts.

Work in Progress

Ongoing projects. Some Portuguese-language manuscripts were prepared for Brazilian policy calls.

Local Grocery Structure and the Incidence of Food-Cost Shocks: Evidence from Fortaleza, Brazil

Work in Progress with Fabrício Carneiro Linhares Urban Economics Food Prices

Studies whether common upstream food-cost shocks have different downstream incidence across neighborhoods within Fortaleza, and whether this heterogeneity is associated with pre-existing local grocery structure.

Decomposing Inequality in Brazil: An Analysis of Gini Coefficient Subgroups

Work in Progress with Ramon Lima Ribeiro and Felipe S. Bastos Inequality Measurement Brazil

Applies a recent exact decomposition of the Gini coefficient to Brazilian household income data, separating within-group and between-group inequality across demographic and regional dimensions.

Productive Public Credit and Municipal Tax Capacity: Evidence from BNDES and Brazilian Fiscal Federalism

Work in Progress with Ramon Lima Ribeiro Portuguese-language manuscript for a Brazilian public policy call Public Finance

Examines whether productive public credit strengthens municipal own-source tax capacity in Brazil, using predetermined municipal exposure to BNDES credit products and national product-level credit shocks.

Credit with Discipline? CAPAG, Federal Guarantees, and Municipal Investment Additionality

Work in Progress with Ramon Lima Ribeiro Portuguese-language manuscript for a Brazilian public policy call Regression Discontinuity

Evaluates Brazil’s municipal Payment Capacity rating as a fiscal-screening and federal-guarantee eligibility rule, exploiting the discontinuity in the current-savings indicator at the institutional cutoff.
